01 // THE BOTTLENECK

The Enterprise Gauntlet

Direct sales to Tier-1 carriers (like ICICI Prudential) face a massive operational mismatch. You are selling agentic AI velocity into a 30-year-old mainframe architecture.

The 18-Month Cycle

Procurement must pass Head of UW, Chief Risk Officer, InfoSec, and IT Infra. A single "data exfiltration" red flag in InfoSec pauses the deal for 6 months.

The Legacy Trap

Underwriters live in AS400 or heavily customized Guidewire instances. They will reject any tool that introduces "dashboard fatigue" outside their core system.

02 // THE THESIS

Why AI Pilots Die

Founders sell "99% extraction accuracy." Enterprise buyers don't care about accuracy; they care about Time-to-Quote (TTQ) and Implementation Friction.

The Accuracy Illusion

Even at 99% accuracy, enterprise compliance mandates a human-in-the-loop review. The cost savings on extraction alone do not justify the integration nightmare.

The Real Moat

The value isn't DocumentCURE (extraction); it's RiskCURE (decisioning). Shifting the pitch from "data entry replacement" to "loss ratio reduction" changes the buyer from IT to the CRO.

03 // THE STRATEGY

The MGA Wedge

Stop doing direct enterprise carrier sales. Pivot GTM to target Managing General Agents (MGAs) and Aggregators to accelerate Gross Written Premium (GWP) processing.

Speed to Market

MGAs lack Tier-1 InfoSec blockers. Sales cycles drop from 18 months to 3 months. MGAs operate on volume and margins, making TTQ a C-level priority.

The Trojan Horse

Once Pibit.ai owns the MGA layer, carriers (who rely on MGAs for distribution) are forced to ingest RiskCURE data natively, bypassing carrier procurement entirely.

04 // EXECUTION

Implementation Playbook

How to deploy RiskCURE without triggering a core system replacement.

Pre-Quote Triage API

Do not attempt deep Guidewire integration on day 1. Deploy via a lightweight API that scores submissions *before* they enter the core system, routing only clean risks to the human underwriter.

On-Premise Guarantees

Package the LLM architecture in a SOC2 Type II compliant VPC that guarantees zero training on carrier data. This kills the InfoSec objection on call #1.
